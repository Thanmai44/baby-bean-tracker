Second Trimester

  • Protein: Essential for growth and development. Good sources include:
    • Lean meats (like chicken and turkey)
    • Fish
    • Eggs
    • Dairy products (such as yogurt and cheese)
    • Legumes (like beans and lentils)
    • Tofu
  • Calcium: Important for bone and teeth development in the baby. Sources include:
    • Dairy products (milk, cheese, yogurt)
    • Fortified plant-based milk (like almond or soy milk)
    • Leafy green vegetables (such as spinach and kale)
    • Calcium-fortified foods (like tofu)
  • Iron: Supports the increase in blood volume and prevents anemia. Foods rich in iron include:
    • Lean red meat
    • Poultry
    • Fish
    • Beans
    • Lentils
    • Tofu
    • Fortified cereals
    • Spinach
  • Omega-3 fatty acids: Aid in brain and vision development in the baby. Sources include:
    • Fatty fish (such as salmon, trout, and sardines)
    • Flaxseeds
    • Chia seeds
    • Walnuts
    • Omega-3 fortified foods
  • Folate (Folic acid): Essential for preventing neural tube defects. Good sources include:
    • Leafy green vegetables (like spinach and kale)
    • Citrus fruits
    • Fortified cereals
    • Beans
    • Lentils
  • Fiber: Helps prevent constipation, a common issue during pregnancy. Sources include:
    • Whole grains (like oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread)
    • Fruits
    • Vegetables
    • Legumes
  • Fluids: Staying hydrated is crucial. Sources include:
    • Water (best choice)
    • Herbal teas
    • Fruit juices (in moderation)
